How Accuracy Is Measured

Every number on this page comes from strictly out-of-sample evaluation. Here is exactly how:

Expanding Window Backtest

The engine uses 21 expanding time windows. Each window trains on historical data only, then tests on future data it has never seen. Window 1 trains on 2013–2014 and tests on 2015. Window 21 trains on 2013–2024 and tests on 2025. No information leaks forward.

Accuracy Methodology

For each discovery, accuracy is computed on every test window using Symmetric Mean Absolute Percentage Error (sMAPE), then converted:

System Average (93.5%)

The headline number is the simple mean of all 332 relationships' accuracies across all 21 windows. Every relationship weighted equally. No cherry-picking, no in-sample inflation.

Market Stress Testing

These 21 windows span COVID-19 (2020), the housing boom (2021–22), aggressive rate hikes (2022–23), and market normalization (2024–25). Accuracy held across all regimes.

Accuracy by Market Category

The engine modeled 68 housing market indicators organized into six categories. Structural metrics (rates, affordability, prices) are extremely well-captured. Market activity metrics (sale-to-list ratios, buyer behavior) are inherently noisier - reflecting the boundary between predictable structure and real-time sentiment. Trivial identity relationships (e.g., predicting a metric from its own rolling average) are excluded.

Key Primary Drivers

These aren't hand-crafted models. The platform discovered these relationships automatically, then validated each one across 21 out-of-sample windows. Every discovery is human-readable and domain-interpretable.

Primary Target - 98.90% OOS Accuracy

price_yoy_change

Price Growth ≈ CPI Inflation × Market Competitiveness²

Home price appreciation is fundamentally driven by the interaction of inflation pressure and market competitiveness. When CPI rises and homes sell near or above asking, prices accelerate. This held across COVID, the 2021-22 boom, rate hikes, and the 2024-25 normalization - 21 independent out-of-sample windows.

Strongest Discovery - 99.90% OOS Accuracy

months_supply

Months Supply ≈ 1.78 + Mortgage Rate

One of the cleanest structural relationships discovered: housing inventory is almost entirely a function of mortgage rates. The ~1.78 intercept represents the irreducible supply floor. At 6% rates, expect ~7.8 months of supply. At 4%, expect ~5.8. This relationship held stable across a decade of wildly different rate environments.

Supply-Demand Link - 99.85% OOS Accuracy

new_home_sales

New Sales ≈ 1070 − 42.5 × (Months Supply + 1.4)

New home sales are a direct linear function of housing inventory. Every additional month of supply destroys ~42.5K in annualized new sales. At 99.85% OOS accuracy across a decade, this is a clean structural relationship: when supply is tight (3 months), sales hit ~880K. When supply bloats to 8 months, sales crash to ~670K.

Inflation Channel - 94.59% OOS Accuracy

rent_yoy_change

Rent Growth ≈ CPI Inflation × 1.15

Rents track inflation with a ~15% amplification factor. When CPI hits 3%, rents grow ~3.45%. This is the most direct inflation transmission channel in real estate. The lower accuracy (94.59%) reflects local rent dynamics that diverge from national CPI trends.

Rate Transmission - 99.86% OOS Accuracy

mortgage_rate_30yr_roll7

30yr Mortgage ≈ Fed Funds + 2.62%

The 30-year mortgage rate is structurally the Fed funds rate plus a ~2.62% credit spread. This held across zero-rate policy (2020), rapid tightening (2022–23), and plateau (2024–25). At 99.86% OOS accuracy, the spread is remarkably stable - mortgage pricing is almost purely a function of Fed policy, not market sentiment.

Construction Pipeline - 99.89% OOS Accuracy

housing_permits

Permits ≈ 2 × (New Sales + 1.73)

Housing permits are almost exactly twice new home sales plus a small offset. This reveals the structural pipeline: the construction industry issues roughly 2 permits for every completed sale (accounting for multi-unit projects, cancellations, and regulatory lead time). A 10% drop in new sales predicts a ~10% permit decline. 99.89% OOS accuracy across a full housing cycle.

What You Can Do With This

This isn't a dashboard - it's a decision engine. Here are the real problems real estate professionals can solve today with DIGINETICS-powered CRE intelligence:

Investment Firms & REITs

Portfolio Allocation & Market Timing

The engine tells you exactly which macro variables drive price growth in each metro. When CPI trends up and sale-to-list tightens, the discovery quantifies the price impact. Use this to:

  • Rank metros by fundamental strength - San Francisco projects +5.50% while Miami projects +4.69%. The gap isn't a guess; it's the mathematical output of their specific CPI and competitiveness conditions.
  • Stress-test rate scenarios - The months supply relationship (99.90% accuracy) tells you: if rates drop from 6% to 5%, supply drops by ~1 month nationally. Feed that into your acquisition model.
  • Identify timing windows - The permits relationship (99.89% accuracy) links construction permits to sales volume. A permit slowdown predicts future supply constraints. Combined with rate projections, it flags metro-specific buying opportunities before they show up in comps.
Lenders & Underwriters

Dynamic Risk Assessment

Move beyond static underwriting models. The platform gives you forward-looking, metro-specific risk signals:

  • Quantified collateral risk - The new home sales discovery (99.85% accuracy) tells you exactly how sales respond to inventory shifts. A 1-month supply increase? Sales drop by ~42.5K annualized. Chain that to the months supply discovery and you can forecast supply → sales → prices from a single rate move.
  • Early warning for distress - The rent growth relationship shows rent tracks CPI x 1.15. When CPI decelerates, you know rental income is about to follow - weeks before it shows up in borrower financials.
  • Portfolio-wide monitoring - Run 31 metros simultaneously. Flag the ones where fundamentals are deteriorating before defaults materialize.
Property Management

Operational Decision Support

Know what's actually driving your market before you set next quarter's strategy:

  • Rent pricing intelligence - Rent growth tracks CPI with a 15% amplifier. When inflation is 2.5%, your rent increases should target ~2.9% to stay competitive. Push harder and you lose occupancy; too conservative and you leave money on the table.
  • Rate impact forecasting - The mortgage-to-Fed-funds discovery (99.86% accuracy) gives you a direct link from Fed policy to mortgage costs. Combine with months supply to project inventory changes from rate decisions before they hit the market.
  • Market-specific intelligence - Each metro has its own dynamics. Austin's price trajectory differs from Boston's for quantifiable reasons. Use metro-specific projections to tailor strategies for each property in your portfolio.
Brokers & Advisors

Data-Backed Market Intelligence

Differentiate your advisory practice with insights no one else has:

  • Quantified pitch decks - "Prices in this metro are projected to grow 5.1% based on current CPI and market competitiveness conditions" beats "we think the market is strong."
  • Causal explanations for clients - When a client asks "why is this market softening?", show them the relationship: months supply = 1.78 + mortgage rate. Rates went up, supply followed. It's math, not opinion.
  • Cross-metro comparison tools - Rank 31 metros on any combination of 84 indicators. Show investors exactly where fundamentals are strongest.
Homebuyers & Sellers

Personal Real Estate Decisions

The same institutional-grade intelligence, accessible to individuals:

  • Is now a good time to buy? - The months supply relationship links inventory directly to mortgage rates. When rates drop, supply tightens and prices follow. The engine quantifies exactly when conditions shift from buyer-favorable to seller-favorable in your metro.
  • Where should I buy? - Compare 31 metros on projected price growth, rent-to-price ratios, and inventory trends. Data-driven relocation decisions.
  • Should I sell or hold? - The price momentum relationship tells you whether growth is accelerating or decelerating in your market. Combined with rate projections, it gives you a quantified hold vs. sell signal.

Methodology: All projections are generated through the DIGINETICS multi-stage analytical pipeline: (1) ingest monthly metro-level housing data from Zillow Research and FRED, (2) engineer 68 market indicators including rolling averages, trends, and derived metrics, (3) discover causal relationships through automated analysis, (4) validate all discoveries across 21 expanding out-of-sample windows spanning 2015–2025, (5) project forward using only validated, stable discoveries. Trivial identity relationships (metrics predicted from their own rolling averages or near-identical cousin metrics like ZHVI ↔ median sale price) are automatically filtered. The system uses no proprietary data - all inputs are freely available from Zillow and FRED. Forward projections represent structural equilibrium estimates based on current market conditions. Actual future market movements will depend on factors including policy changes, economic shocks, and local market dynamics not captured in historical patterns.
