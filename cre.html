Home price appreciation is fundamentally driven by the interaction of inflation pressure and market competitiveness. When CPI rises and homes sell near or above asking, prices accelerate. Strongest Discovery — 99.90% OOS Accuracy

months_supply

1.7828 + mortgage_rate_30yr

Months Supply ≈ 1.78 + Mortgage Rate

One of the cleanest structural relationships discovered: housing inventory is almost entirely a function of mortgage rates. The ~1.78 intercept represents the irreducible supply floor. At 6% rates, expect ~7.8 months of supply. At 4%, expect ~5.8. This equation held stable across a decade of wildly different rate environments.

Home Value ≈ Median Sale Price + 2 × Unemployment (smoothed)

Zillow Home Value Index tracks median sale prices with an unemployment adjustment. Higher unemployment depresses valuations relative to transaction prices — a risk premium the market prices in. At 99.94% OOS accuracy, this is a near-perfect structural identity.

Rent Growth ≈ CPI Inflation × 1.15

Rents track inflation with a ~15% amplification factor. When CPI hits 3%, rents grow ~3.45%. This is the most direct inflation transmission channel in real estate. The lower accuracy (94.59%) reflects local rent dynamics that diverge from national CPI trends.

What You Can Do With This

This isn't a dashboard — it's a decision engine. Here are the real problems real estate professionals can solve today:

Investment Firms & REITs

Portfolio Allocation & Market Timing

Know exactly which macro variables drive price growth in each metro. When CPI trends up and sale-to-list tightens, the equation quantifies the price impact. Use this to:

  • Rank metros by fundamental strength — San Francisco projects +5.50% while Miami projects +4.69%. The gap isn't a guess; it's the mathematical output of their specific CPI and competitiveness conditions.
  • Stress-test rate scenarios — The months supply equation (99.90% accuracy) tells you: if rates drop from 6% to 5%, supply drops by ~1 month nationally. Feed that into your acquisition model.
  • Identify timing windows — The affordability equation tracks a persistent downward drift. When affordability hits threshold levels, the model flags metro-specific buying opportunities before they show up in comps.
Lenders & Underwriters

Dynamic Risk Assessment

Move beyond static underwriting models with forward-looking, metro-specific risk signals:

  • Quantified collateral risk — The ZHVI equation (99.94% accuracy) tells you exactly how home values respond to unemployment changes. A 1% unemployment spike in a metro? The equation tells you the dollar impact on collateral value.
  • Early warning for distress — The rent growth equation shows rent tracks CPI x 1.15. When CPI decelerates, you know rental income is about to follow — weeks before it shows up in borrower financials.
  • Portfolio-wide monitoring — Run 31 metros simultaneously. Flag the ones where fundamentals are deteriorating before defaults materialize.
Property Management

Operational Decision Support

Know what's actually driving your market before you set next quarter's strategy:

  • Rent pricing intelligence — Rent growth tracks CPI with a 15% amplifier. When inflation is 2.5%, your rent increases should target ~2.9% to stay competitive. Push harder and you lose occupancy; too conservative and you leave money on the table.
  • Cap rate forecasting — At 100% OOS accuracy, the cap rate equation gives you a reliable forward signal for property valuations. Use it to time capex decisions, refinancing, and disposition strategies.
  • Market-specific intelligence — Each metro has its own dynamics. Austin's price trajectory differs from Boston's for quantifiable reasons. Use metro-specific projections to tailor strategies for each property in your portfolio.
Brokers & Advisors

Data-Backed Market Intelligence

Differentiate your advisory practice with insights no one else has:

  • Quantified pitch decks — "Prices in this metro are projected to grow 5.1% based on current CPI and market competitiveness conditions" beats "we think the market is strong."
  • Causal explanations for clients — When a client asks "why is this market softening?", show them the equation: months supply = 1.78 + mortgage rate. Rates went up, supply followed. It's math, not opinion.
  • Cross-metro comparison tools — Rank 31 metros on any combination of 84 indicators. Show investors exactly where fundamentals are strongest.
Homebuyers & Sellers

Personal Real Estate Decisions

The same institutional-grade intelligence, accessible to individuals:

  • Is now a good time to buy? — The affordability index tracks a persistent downward drift. Quantify exactly how much affordability is changing in your metro and whether current conditions favor buyers or sellers.
  • Where should I buy? — Compare 31 metros on projected price growth, rent-to-price ratios, and inventory trends. Data-driven relocation decisions.
  • Should I sell or hold? — The price momentum equation tells you whether growth is accelerating or decelerating in your market. Combined with rate projections, it gives you a quantified hold vs. sell signal.
