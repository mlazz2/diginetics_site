Why This Is Feasible

What we've already proven that applies directly to CRE.

Commercial real estate is a time-series prediction problem with causal relationships between metrics. That's exactly the problem structure our platform has already solved three times.

Leading Indicator Discovery: Already Proven

In retail, the platform discovered that disposable vape average sale predicts store-wide revenue 3 days in advance, and glass average sale predicts it 10 days out. CRE has analogous leading indicators: vacancy rate changes, lease renewal velocity, and tenant credit shifts all precede NOI movements. The platform is designed to find these timing relationships automatically.

Hidden Dependency Mapping: Already Proven

In retail, cigarette sales turned out to depend on vape traffic, not cigarette demand. In CRE, office valuations may depend on remote-work adoption rates in the tenant base, not office demand metrics. The platform's ability to discover non-obvious cross-variable dependencies is what makes it valuable for CRE, where traditional comps miss structural drivers.

Per-Entity Adaptation: Already Proven

The platform produced unique performance drivers for each of 60 MLB players without being told what type of player it was analyzing. A Class A office in Manhattan and a suburban warehouse in Tampa are fundamentally different assets. The platform's ability to adapt per-entity means each property gets its own causal model, not a generic sector average.

Regime Transition Detection: Already Proven

The platform detected 73,076 regime transitions across 60 baseball players, identifying tipping points between hot streaks and slumps. CRE markets undergo analogous regime shifts: from buyer's to seller's markets, from cap rate compression to expansion, from occupancy growth to contraction. We're testing whether the platform detects these transitions with the same precision.

Compound Interactions: Already Proven

24% of discovered relationships in baseball were multi-factor interactions invisible to single-variable analysis. CRE valuations are notoriously multi-factor: a property's value depends on the interaction between interest rates, local employment, tenant mix, and lease terms simultaneously. Single-variable regression misses these. Our platform is built to find them.

Zero Code Changes: Already Proven

The same platform went from baseball to retail to healthcare with only a configuration change. CRE is next. The data structure is different (property-level time series instead of player-level), but the underlying prediction problem is the same: discover causal relationships, find leading indicators, project forward, explain why.

Specific Areas We're Researching

Multifamily Rent Prediction

We're investigating whether local employment data, migration patterns, and permit activity serve as leading indicators for effective rent changes at the property level. The same causal-chain discovery that mapped pitch environment to batting outcomes should map macro indicators to rent outcomes.

Office Occupancy Regime Shifts

Post-pandemic office markets are experiencing structural regime changes. We're testing whether the platform can detect when a submarket is transitioning between occupancy regimes and predict how far the shift will go, using the same regime-detection capability proven across 73,076 transitions in baseball.

Industrial/Logistics Demand Cycles

E-commerce penetration rates, port throughput, and inventory-to-sales ratios may serve as upstream drivers for industrial property demand. We're testing whether these relationships hold with quantifiable time delays, the same way retail revenue shifts followed specific category signals by 3-10 days.

Cap Rate Sensitivity Analysis

Cap rates are influenced by interest rates, risk premiums, and growth expectations simultaneously. We're researching whether the platform's compound-interaction discovery can identify which combinations of macro factors drive cap rate movements for specific property types, rather than relying on generic interest-rate correlations.

Tenant Credit Risk Cascades

A single tenant default can cascade through a property's financials. We're investigating whether tenant-level financial signals serve as leading indicators for property-level NOI shifts, analogous to how individual metric shifts in baseball cascade through the performance chain.

Climate & ESG Risk Pricing

Flood risk, energy efficiency scores, and ESG compliance are increasingly priced into CRE valuations. We're testing whether the platform can quantify exactly how much these factors move value for specific property types in specific markets, replacing qualitative ESG narratives with quantified impact.
