Healthcare

Patient Monitoring & Clinical Outcomes

The same engine that discovered causal relationships in baseball and retail is configured and ready for healthcare patient data. Results will be published here once testing is complete.

Results Coming Soon

The healthcare configuration is built and validated. We're preparing compliant patient datasets for testing. Results will be published here with full methodology, equations, and accuracy metrics.

What We're Testing

The healthcare configuration tracks 24 metrics organized in a 3-layer causal hierarchy, designed to discover how upstream patient indicators propagate through intermediate markers to drive clinical outcomes.

Layer 1: Vital Signs (6 metrics)

Heart rate, systolic blood pressure, diastolic blood pressure, body temperature, respiratory rate, oxygen saturation. These are the leading indicators — the earliest signals of patient state changes.

Layer 2: Lab Values & Behavior (11 metrics)

Glucose, cholesterol (total, LDL, HDL), hemoglobin, creatinine, medication adherence, medication dosage, daily step count, sleep hours, exercise minutes. Intermediate metrics that connect vitals to outcomes.

Layer 3: Clinical Outcomes (7 metrics)

BMI, weight, pain score, quality of life score, hospital readmission risk, complication rate, recovery rate. The outcomes we're predicting and tracing causal drivers for.

What We Expect to Find

Based on the system's performance in baseball (263 feedback loops from 58 metrics) and retail (30 feedback loops from 98 metrics), we expect the healthcare configuration to discover:

Causal chains to readmission

Which vital sign changes predict readmission risk, through which intermediate lab values, with what time delays? The system will trace the full chain — not just "these correlate" but "vital A changes, which shifts lab B after X days, which drives readmission risk Y days later."

Medication adherence impact

How does medication adherence causally affect each downstream metric? The system's constraint framework enforces that higher adherence should improve outcomes (monotonicity constraint) — but the specific magnitudes and time delays will be discovered from data.

Activity → outcome equations

Readable equations showing exactly how step count, sleep, and exercise relate to BMI, pain, and recovery rate. Not "exercise is good" — but "each additional 1,000 daily steps corresponds to X change in recovery rate after Y days."
