What We're Investigating

Claims Frequency Modeling

We're testing whether the platform can discover which behavioral patterns and external factors are genuine leading indicators of claims activity vs. noise, predicting which policyholders are most likely to file claims and when.

Loss Ratio Drivers

Which factors actually drive loss ratios for specific product lines? We're researching whether the platform can discover specific, quantified relationships between policyholder attributes, market conditions, and claims outcomes that go beyond generic actuarial tables.

Risk Segmentation

Just as the platform found that different MLB players need completely different strategies, we're testing whether it discovers natural risk segments in policyholder data that don't map to pre-defined categories.

Why This Is Feasible

What we've already proven that applies directly to insurance.

Insurance is fundamentally a prediction problem: estimate future claims from historical data. Our healthcare deployment already solved a closely related problem on 101,766 real patient encounters.

Healthcare Rate Prediction: Already Proven

The platform predicted hospital readmission rates within 0.02 percentage points on 101,766 patient encounters. Insurance claims prediction is structurally identical: predict a rate (claims frequency) from a population (policyholders) using historical data (claims history + risk factors). The 0.18% error achieved in healthcare demonstrates the platform's capability on exactly this type of problem.

Autonomous Risk Driver Discovery: Already Proven

In healthcare, the platform independently discovered that high glucose prevalence and demographic factors drive readmission, without being given any clinical hypotheses. For insurance, we're testing whether it can autonomously discover which policyholder attributes, behavioral patterns, and external factors drive claims, without needing actuaries to specify what to look for.

Per-Entity Risk Profiles: Already Proven

60 MLB players each received unique performance drivers. Different policyholders have fundamentally different risk profiles driven by different factors. We're testing whether the platform produces per-segment (and eventually per-policy) risk models that identify the specific drivers for each risk class automatically.

Explainability for Regulators: Already Proven

Every prediction the platform makes comes with a transparent causal explanation. Insurance regulators (Solvency II, IFRS 17, state DOIs) increasingly require model explainability. The platform doesn't produce black-box scores. It produces specific, auditable reasoning: "this policy is high risk because of X, Y, and Z, with these specific magnitudes."

Compound Risk Interactions: Already Proven

24% of discovered relationships in baseball were multi-factor interactions. Insurance risk is notoriously multi-factor: a policyholder's risk depends on the interaction between age, location, claims history, coverage type, and external conditions simultaneously. Single-factor actuarial models miss these. We're testing whether the platform captures compound risk interactions that improve pricing accuracy.

Regime Transition Detection: Already Proven

73,076 regime transitions detected in baseball. Insurance portfolios undergo analogous regime shifts: a hard market transitions to soft, catastrophe frequency changes, and loss development patterns shift. We're researching whether the platform detects these transitions early enough to inform reserve adjustments and pricing changes before losses materialize.

Specific Areas We're Researching

Auto Insurance: Telematics-Based Risk

We're testing whether driving behavior data (hard braking, speed patterns, time-of-day driving) serves as a genuine leading indicator for claims frequency, or whether some commonly tracked telematics signals are correlated noise. The platform is designed to separate true causal drivers from coincidental patterns.

Homeowners: Climate Risk Pricing

Climate change is repricing property risk in real time. We're investigating whether the platform can quantify how specific climate indicators (flood frequency, wildfire proximity, hail corridor exposure) transmit to claims severity for specific property types in specific geographies, replacing broad actuarial zones with per-property risk signals.

Life Insurance: Mortality Driver Discovery

We're researching whether the platform can discover which combinations of medical, behavioral, and socioeconomic factors are genuine mortality predictors for specific age cohorts. The same platform that found clinical readmission drivers in healthcare data should discover mortality drivers in life insurance data.

Workers' Compensation: Industry-Specific Claims Patterns

Different industries have fundamentally different injury profiles. We're testing whether the platform discovers industry-specific claims drivers that improve loss prediction beyond generic NCCI class codes, identifying which workplace conditions actually lead to claims vs. which are theoretical risks.

Reinsurance: Catastrophe Correlation Discovery

Catastrophe risk is driven by correlations between perils that traditional models may not capture. We're investigating whether the platform can discover hidden correlations between natural catastrophe events, economic conditions, and aggregate loss patterns across cedant portfolios.

Fraud Detection: Behavioral Leading Indicators

We're testing whether the platform can identify which sequences of policyholder behaviors serve as leading indicators for fraudulent claims, discovering the behavioral "attack chains" (analogous to the causal chains it discovered in baseball) that precede fraudulent filings.

Business Applications

P&C Carriers

Per-policy risk scoring with explainable drivers. Know exactly why a policy is rated high or low risk, with specific factors and magnitudes. Differentiate pricing based on discovered signals competitors can't see.

Life & Health Insurance

Discover which clinical and behavioral factors actually predict mortality and morbidity risk. The same platform that found high glucose prevalence as a readmission driver in healthcare applies directly to life underwriting.

Reinsurance

Portfolio-level risk assessment with per-segment driver analysis. Identify which cedant portfolios have hidden correlated risks and quantify aggregate exposure to regime shifts before they hit loss ratios.

Regulatory & Compliance

Every prediction and pricing decision comes with transparent, auditable reasoning. Built for Solvency II, IFRS 17, and state regulatory requirements. Replace "the model says so" with "here's exactly what drives this price."
