Why This Is Feasible

What we've already proven that applies directly to insurance.

Insurance is fundamentally a prediction problem: estimate future claims from historical data. Our healthcare deployment already solved a closely related problem on 101,766 real patient encounters.

Healthcare Rate Prediction: Already Proven

The platform predicted hospital readmission rates within 0.02 percentage points on 101,766 patient encounters. Insurance claims prediction is structurally identical: predict a rate (claims frequency) from a population (policyholders) using historical data (claims history + risk factors). The 0.18% error achieved in healthcare demonstrates the platform's capability on exactly this type of problem.

Autonomous Risk Driver Discovery: Already Proven

In healthcare, the platform independently discovered that high glucose prevalence and demographic factors drive readmission, without being given any clinical hypotheses. This proven capability could apply to insurance, autonomously discovering which policyholder attributes, behavioral patterns, and external factors drive claims, without needing actuaries to specify what to look for.

Per-Entity Risk Profiles: Already Proven

60 MLB players each received unique performance drivers. Different policyholders have fundamentally different risk profiles driven by different factors. This proven capability suggests the platform could produce per-segment (and eventually per-policy) risk models that identify the specific drivers for each risk class automatically.

Explainability for Regulators: Already Proven

Every prediction the platform makes comes with a transparent causal explanation. Insurance regulators (Solvency II, IFRS 17, state DOIs) increasingly require model explainability. The platform doesn't produce black-box scores. It produces specific, auditable reasoning: "this policy is high risk because of X, Y, and Z, with these specific magnitudes."

Compound Risk Interactions: Already Proven

24% of discovered relationships in baseball were multi-factor interactions. Insurance risk is notoriously multi-factor: a policyholder's risk depends on the interaction between age, location, claims history, coverage type, and external conditions simultaneously. Single-factor actuarial models miss these. This suggests the platform could capture compound risk interactions that improve pricing accuracy.

Regime Transition Detection: Already Proven

73,076 regime transitions detected in baseball. Insurance portfolios undergo analogous regime shifts: a hard market transitions to soft, catastrophe frequency changes, and loss development patterns shift. This proven capability suggests the platform could detect these transitions early enough to inform reserve adjustments and pricing changes before losses materialize.

Where This Could Apply

Auto Insurance: Telematics-Based Risk

Driving behavior data (hard braking, speed patterns, time-of-day driving) could serve as a genuine leading indicator for claims frequency, or some commonly tracked telematics signals may be correlated noise. Based on proven results separating true drivers from coincidence, the platform could distinguish causal factors from spurious patterns in telematics data.

Homeowners: Climate Risk Pricing

Climate change is repricing property risk in real time. The platform's proven ability to quantify causal relationships suggests it could measure how specific climate indicators (flood frequency, wildfire proximity, hail corridor exposure) transmit to claims severity for specific property types in specific geographies, replacing broad actuarial zones with per-property risk signals.

Life Insurance: Mortality Driver Discovery

Based on proven results discovering clinical readmission drivers in healthcare data, the platform could discover which combinations of medical, behavioral, and socioeconomic factors are genuine mortality predictors for specific age cohorts in life insurance data.

Workers' Compensation: Industry-Specific Claims Patterns

Different industries have fundamentally different injury profiles. The platform's proven ability to build per-entity models suggests it could discover industry-specific claims drivers that improve loss prediction beyond generic NCCI class codes, identifying which workplace conditions actually lead to claims vs. which are theoretical risks.

Reinsurance: Catastrophe Correlation Discovery

Catastrophe risk is driven by correlations between perils that traditional models may not capture. The platform's proven compound-interaction discovery suggests it could find hidden correlations between natural catastrophe events, economic conditions, and aggregate loss patterns across cedant portfolios.

Fraud Detection: Behavioral Leading Indicators

Based on proven results discovering causal chains in baseball, the platform could identify which sequences of policyholder behaviors serve as leading indicators for fraudulent claims, discovering the behavioral "attack chains" that precede fraudulent filings.
