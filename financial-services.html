Why This Is Feasible

What we've already proven that could apply directly to finance.

Financial markets are time-series data with causal relationships, feedback loops, and regime transitions. That's exactly the problem structure we've solved in three other domains.

Causal Chain Discovery: Already Proven

The platform independently discovered a 4-layer causal chain in baseball: pitch environment → swing decisions → contact quality → outcomes. Financial markets have analogous chains: macro conditions → sector rotation → earnings expectations → price movements. This proven capability suggests the platform could discover these hierarchies from financial data without being told what to look for.

Feedback Loops & Momentum: Already Proven

The platform discovered 30,141 self-reinforcing feedback loops in baseball, explaining why hot streaks compound and slumps spiral. Financial markets exhibit the same dynamics: momentum effects, mean-reversion cycles, and volatility clustering. This suggests the platform could map these loops with similar precision in equity and fixed-income data.

Regime Transition Detection: Already Proven

73,076 regime transitions detected across 60 players: tipping points between performance phases. Financial markets undergo analogous regime shifts: risk-on to risk-off, growth to value rotation, low-volatility to high-volatility environments. This proven capability could be valuable for early detection of market regime transitions.

Per-Entity Adaptation: Already Proven

Each of 60 MLB players received unique performance drivers without the platform being told their archetype. Apple and ExxonMobil are driven by fundamentally different factors. This suggests the platform could produce per-company driver models that adapt to each equity's unique characteristics automatically.

Compound Factor Interactions: Already Proven

24% of discovered relationships in baseball were multi-factor interactions invisible to single-variable analysis. Factor investing suffers the same limitation: traditional factor models test one factor at a time. This proven capability could discover factor interactions (e.g., value + momentum combined) that single-factor models miss.

Explainability Built In: Already Proven

Every prediction the platform makes comes with a clear causal explanation of what's driving it. In healthcare, it explained that high glucose prevalence drives readmission. Financial regulators (MiFID II, SEC) increasingly require prediction explainability. This suggests the platform's architecture could naturally satisfy audit-ready reasoning requirements.
