Why This Is Feasible

What we've already proven that applies directly to finance.

Financial markets are time-series data with causal relationships, feedback loops, and regime transitions. That's exactly the problem structure we've solved in three other domains.

Causal Chain Discovery: Already Proven

The platform independently discovered a 4-layer causal chain in baseball: pitch environment → swing decisions → contact quality → outcomes. Financial markets have analogous chains: macro conditions → sector rotation → earnings expectations → price movements. The platform is designed to discover these hierarchies from data without being told what to look for.

Feedback Loops & Momentum: Already Proven

The platform discovered 30,141 self-reinforcing feedback loops in baseball, explaining why hot streaks compound and slumps spiral. Financial markets exhibit the same dynamics: momentum effects, mean-reversion cycles, and volatility clustering. We're testing whether the platform maps these loops with the same precision in equity and fixed-income data.

Regime Transition Detection: Already Proven

73,076 regime transitions detected across 60 players: tipping points between performance phases. Financial markets undergo analogous regime shifts: risk-on to risk-off, growth to value rotation, low-volatility to high-volatility environments. The platform's ability to detect these transitions early is what makes it potentially valuable for portfolio management.

Per-Entity Adaptation: Already Proven

Each of 60 MLB players received unique performance drivers without the platform being told their archetype. Apple and ExxonMobil are driven by fundamentally different factors. We're testing whether the platform produces per-company driver models that adapt to each equity's unique characteristics automatically.

Compound Factor Interactions: Already Proven

24% of discovered relationships in baseball were multi-factor interactions invisible to single-variable analysis. Factor investing suffers the same limitation: traditional factor models test one factor at a time. We're researching whether the platform discovers factor interactions (e.g., value + momentum combined) that single-factor models miss.

Explainability Built In: Already Proven

Every prediction the platform makes comes with a clear causal explanation of what's driving it. In healthcare, it explained that high glucose prevalence drives readmission. Financial regulators (MiFID II, SEC) increasingly require prediction explainability. The platform's architecture inherently produces audit-ready reasoning.

Specific Areas We're Researching

Earnings Surprise Prediction

We're testing whether alternative data signals (supply chain metrics, web traffic, job postings) serve as leading indicators for earnings beats and misses, with the same quantified time delays the platform discovered in retail revenue prediction.

Credit Spread Drivers

Credit spreads are influenced by default risk, liquidity, and macro sentiment simultaneously. We're investigating whether the platform's compound-interaction discovery can identify which combinations of factors drive spread movements for specific issuers and sectors.

Sector Rotation Timing

When does money flow from growth to value, from domestic to international? We're testing whether the platform can detect these rotation signals early by discovering leading indicators in cross-sector fund flows, yield curve movements, and economic data releases.

Volatility Regime Prediction

Low-volatility regimes transition to high-volatility regimes at specific tipping points. We're researching whether the platform's regime-detection capability (proven across 73,076 transitions in baseball) can identify these tipping points in VIX and realized volatility data.

ESG Factor Materiality

Which ESG factors actually impact financial performance for which companies? We're testing whether the platform can separate ESG signals that genuinely predict returns from those that are correlated noise, providing the quantified materiality analysis investors need.

Macro-to-Micro Transmission

How do Federal Reserve decisions transmit through to individual company earnings, and with what delay? We're investigating whether the platform can map the specific transmission chain from macro policy changes to company-level financial impacts.
