Why This Is Feasible

What we've already proven that applies directly to cybersecurity.

Security threats follow structured patterns with leading indicators. That's the same problem structure the platform has already solved in three other domains.

Track Record: Proven Across Industries

In baseball, we identified structured performance patterns. Cyber attacks follow analogous structures: reconnaissance → initial access → lateral movement → exfiltration. This suggests we could discover these attack patterns from raw telemetry without being told what to look for.

Leading Indicator Discovery: Already Proven

In retail, we found specific metrics that predict revenue shifts 3-10 days in advance. Security incidents are preceded by anomalous behaviors (unusual login patterns, data access spikes, configuration changes) that occur hours or days before a breach escalates. This could apply to discovering these pre-breach signals with the same quantified timing.

Signal vs. Noise Separation: Already Proven

The platform found that hookah sales are noise (self-contained, no cross-department impact) while vape metrics are genuine signals. SOCs are drowning in alerts where 95%+ are false positives. This suggests we could separate genuine threat signals from benign noise with the same precision it achieved in retail data.

Per-Environment Adaptation: Already Proven

60 MLB players each received unique risk profiles. Every network environment is different. This suggests we could produce per-environment threat models that adapt to each organization's specific architecture, traffic patterns, and attack surface without manual rule-writing.

Multi-Factor Analysis: Already Proven

24% of baseball relationships were multi-factor. Security breaches are rarely single-cause: a misconfiguration combined with an unpatched vulnerability combined with a phishing success creates compound risk. This could apply to discovering which combinations of security gaps create actual breach risk vs. theoretical exposure.

Explainable Risk Scoring: Already Proven

Every platform prediction comes with transparent reasoning. In healthcare, it explained exactly why readmission rates behave the way they do. For cybersecurity, this means every risk score could come with a specific explanation of what's driving it—critical for audit trails, compliance reporting, and analyst decision-making.

Regulatory Frameworks This Could Cover

HIPAA (Health Insurance Portability & Accountability Act)

Protected health information (PHI) is one of the most heavily regulated data categories. We could analyze access patterns, encryption status, audit log coverage, and data flow mapping to identify which specific compliance gaps carry the highest enforcement risk. Law firms could quantify exposure for healthcare clients instead of relying on generic risk assessments.

GDPR (General Data Protection Regulation)

Fines up to 4% of global annual revenue. We could identify which data processing activities, consent mechanisms, and cross-border data flows create the highest enforcement exposure—discovering which GDPR articles a company is most likely to violate based on their specific data architecture and operational patterns.

CCPA / CPRA (California Consumer Privacy Act)

Consumer data rights and opt-out requirements create complex compliance obligations. We could map data collection practices against CCPA requirements, identifying which consumer touchpoints carry the most risk and where data retention policies create unnecessary exposure.

SOX (Sarbanes-Oxley Act)

Financial data integrity and internal controls. We could analyze access patterns to financial systems, change management logs, and separation-of-duties enforcement to identify which SOX controls are genuinely effective vs. creating compliance theater without reducing actual risk.

PCI DSS (Payment Card Industry Data Security Standard)

Cardholder data protection across all 12 PCI DSS requirement families. We could identify which requirements are most frequently the root cause of breaches in specific industries and which compensating controls actually reduce risk vs. which are paper exercises.

State Privacy Laws (VCDPA, CPA, CTDPA, and more)

The patchwork of state-level privacy legislation creates overlapping obligations. We could map a company's data practices against all applicable state requirements simultaneously, identifying where a single change can resolve multiple compliance gaps at once.
