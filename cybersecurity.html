Regulatory Frameworks We Cover

HIPAA (Health Insurance Portability & Accountability Act)

Protected health information (PHI) is one of the most heavily regulated data categories. The platform analyzes access patterns, encryption status, audit log coverage, and data flow mapping to identify which specific compliance gaps carry the highest enforcement risk. Law firms can quantify exposure for healthcare clients instead of relying on generic risk assessments.

GDPR (General Data Protection Regulation)

Fines up to 4% of global annual revenue. The platform identifies which data processing activities, consent mechanisms, and cross-border data flows create the highest enforcement exposure. Discover which GDPR articles a company is most likely to violate based on their specific data architecture and operational patterns.

CCPA / CPRA (California Consumer Privacy Act)

Consumer data rights and opt-out requirements create complex compliance obligations. The platform maps data collection practices against CCPA requirements, identifying which consumer touchpoints carry the most risk and where data retention policies create unnecessary exposure.

SOX (Sarbanes-Oxley Act)

Financial data integrity and internal controls. The platform analyzes access patterns to financial systems, change management logs, and separation-of-duties enforcement to identify which SOX controls are genuinely effective vs. creating compliance theater without reducing actual risk.

PCI DSS (Payment Card Industry Data Security Standard)

Cardholder data protection across all 12 PCI DSS requirement families. The platform identifies which requirements are most frequently the root cause of breaches in specific industries and which compensating controls actually reduce risk vs. which are paper exercises.

State Privacy Laws (VCDPA, CPA, CTDPA, and more)

The patchwork of state-level privacy legislation creates overlapping obligations. The platform maps a company's data practices against all applicable state requirements simultaneously, identifying where a single change can resolve multiple compliance gaps at once.

What the Platform Delivers for Legal Teams

Quantified Compliance Gap Analysis

Not just "you're non-compliant." The platform identifies which specific gaps carry the highest enforcement risk based on discovered patterns in regulatory actions, breach data, and penalty history. Prioritize remediation by actual risk, not checklist order.

Breach Impact Prediction

When a breach occurs, how large will the regulatory exposure be? The platform models likely penalty ranges based on the type of data exposed, notification timelines, remediation speed, and the specific regulatory framework involved. Law firms can advise clients on expected financial exposure within hours of discovery.

Enforcement Pattern Analysis

Which types of companies are regulators targeting? Which violations trigger the largest penalties? The platform analyzes enforcement action history across HIPAA, GDPR, CCPA, and SOX to identify which compliance failures regulators are prioritizing in the current enforcement climate.

Cross-Regulation Conflict Detection

Data retention requirements under one regulation can conflict with deletion mandates under another. The platform maps all applicable regulatory obligations for a client and identifies where requirements conflict, giving legal teams the analysis they need to advise on the safest path.

Due Diligence for M&A

Acquiring a company means acquiring their compliance liabilities. The platform assesses target company data practices against all applicable regulations, quantifying hidden compliance debt that should be factored into valuation and deal structure.

Incident Response Readiness Scoring

Is your client prepared for the 72-hour GDPR notification window? Does their HIPAA breach response plan actually work? The platform scores incident response readiness across each regulatory framework, identifying where response capabilities fall short before a breach tests them.
